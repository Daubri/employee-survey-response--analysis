Employee Survey Response Analysis

📊 Project Overview
An organization wanted to better understand employee satisfaction across departments and employee roles. Using employee survey response data, I built an interactive Excel dashboard to identify key satisfaction drivers, uncover trends, and provide actionable recommendations that could support management decisions.



⸻

🎯 Business Problem

The HR team needed answers to three key questions:

* Which survey questions received the highest and lowest ratings?
* Which departments and employee roles showed the strongest and weakest satisfaction levels?
* What practical actions could management take to improve employee satisfaction?


⸻

📁 Dataset

* Employee Survey Responses
* Over 14,000 survey responses
* Data cleaned and analyzed using Microsoft Excel

⸻

🛠 Tools Used

* Microsoft Excel
* Power Query
* Pivot Tables
* Pivot Charts
* Dashboard Design

⸻

📈 Dashboard Features

The dashboard includes:

* Overall Average Survey Score
* Highest Question Score
* Lowest Question Score
* Lowest Performing Department
* Average Survey Score by Department
* Average Survey Score by Employee Role
* Average Survey Score by Question
* Interactive Department and Employee Role Filters

⸻

🔍 Key Insights

* The overall employee satisfaction score was 2.98, indicating room for improvement.
* Employees clearly understood what was expected of them at work, making it the highest-rated survey statement.
* Opportunities for learning and career growth received some of the lowest ratings.
* Satisfaction levels differed across departments.
* Supervisors and managers generally reported slightly higher satisfaction than other employee groups.

⸻

💼 Business Recommendations

* Invest in employee development and career growth programs.
* Strengthen recognition and reward initiatives.
* Improve communication between management and employees.
* Conduct regular employee feedback sessions.
* Provide additional support to departments with lower satisfaction scores.

⸻

⸻

🗣 Communication Approach

Rather than overwhelming stakeholders with statistics, this dashboard focuses on answering three important business questions:

1. What are employees most satisfied with?
2. Where are employees least satisfied?
3. What actions should management take to improve employee satisfaction?

The goal was to make the dashboard easy for non-technical decision-makers to understand.
